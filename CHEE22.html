Project Overview

CHEE22 is a local two-player game for the PlayStation 5 that takes the foundations of chess and reimagines them into something more playful and contained. At the time of developing Balatro was booming in popularity so we were inspired to try something similar — taking something familiar and making it our own. Six of us built it over a single semester at Sheffield Hallam University: five programmers and one designer, all working on a shared C++ engine assembled from each member's individual codebase.

It was graded First Class. For me personally, this was the first time touching a PS5 development kit, which meant getting to grips with Sony's proprietary toolchain and TRC compliance alongside the usual game development challenges.

I got tasked with the audio integration using an existing library on the PS5 devkit and contributed to the final polish of the level editor.

Key Features

Local Multiplayer

Supports local multiplayer with one or multiple controllers.

PBR

Simulate realistic lighting and surface interactions for lifelike visuals.

Multithreaded 3D Audio

Implement multithreaded audio processing for immersive 3D sound experiences.

Assets Integration

Integrated colour theme and created 3D assets.
